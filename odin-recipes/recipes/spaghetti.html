Spaghetti

Description

Spaghetti is a beloved Italian pasta dish known for its long, thin, cylindrical noodles typically made from durum wheat semolina. This classic dish is often paired with a variety of sauces, such as marinara (tomato-based), Bolognese (meat-based), Alfredo (cream-based), or aglio e olio (garlic and olive oil), among others.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti pasta
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Olive oil (optional)
  • Marinara sauce (or any sauce of your choice)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)
  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Fill a large pot with water, leaving enough room for the spaghetti to cook without sticking together.
  2. Add a generous amount of salt to the water. The water should taste like seawater; this helps flavor the pasta.
  3. Place the pot of water on the stove over high heat and bring it to a rolling boil.
  4. Once the water is boiling, carefully add the spaghetti to the pot, using tongs or a pasta fork to gently separate the strands.
  5. Stir the spaghetti occasionally to prevent sticking and cook according to the package instructions or until it reaches the desired level of tenderness (usually around 8-10 minutes for al dente).
  6. Once the spaghetti is cooked to your liking, turn off the heat and carefully drain it in a colander placed in the sink. Be cautious of the hot steam.
  7. Return the drained spaghetti to the pot and drizzle with a bit of olive oil, if desired, to prevent sticking. Toss gently to coat.
  8. Pour the desired amount of marinara sauce (or any sauce of your choice) over the spaghetti and toss until evenly coated.
  9. Enjoy!
